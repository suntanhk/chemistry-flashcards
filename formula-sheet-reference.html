Mole Concept
MOSTLY ON SPEC
▾

Matches Topic 5 (Formulae, Equations & Amount of Substance) closely — molality and normality are not Edexcel terms, marked below.

ON SPEC
Moles (n)
n = Mass (g) ÷ Molar mass (g/mol)
ON SPEC
Number of particles
N = n × NA (NA = 6.022×10²³ mol⁻¹)
ON SPEC
Molarity / concentration (c)
c = moles solute ÷ volume solution (dm³)
NOT EDEXCEL
Molality (m)
m = moles solute ÷ mass solvent (kg) — not an Edexcel term
NOT EDEXCEL
Normality (N)
N = equivalents of solute ÷ volume solution (L) — not an Edexcel term
ON SPEC
Mass %
(mass of solute ÷ mass of solution) × 100
ON SPEC
Mole fraction (XA)
XA = nA ÷ (nA + nB)

Gas Laws
ON SPEC
▾

Matches Topic 5.5 (Gas Volume Calculations). Edexcel uses SI units for pV=nRT: p in Pa, V in m³, R = 8.31 J K⁻¹ mol⁻¹ — not the L·atm units shown here.

ON SPEC
Boyle's Law (T constant)
P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
ON SPEC
Charles' Law (P constant)
V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
ON SPEC
Avogadro's Law (P,T constant)
V₁/n₁ = V₂/n₂
ON SPEC
Ideal Gas Equation
pV = nRT (Edexcel: p in Pa, V in m³, R = 8.31 J K⁻¹ mol⁻¹, T in K)
